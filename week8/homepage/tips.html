Rider tips

Learn counter steering. Counter steering as a beginner can be a bit counter intuitive (pun intended). It works like this: When you're travelling at speed on the motorcycle, tilt the handlebars slightly to the left. This can be done both by pushing on the right side of the handlebar or pulling on the left side, or both. This will make the bike want to turn right, the OPPOSITE direction of the direction you made the handlebars go. Giving the handlebars a small nudge will make the bike want to initiate a turn in the other direction. This is way easier than using force or your bodyweight to make the bike turn, and allows for faster turns and more fine grained control of the bike.

Loud pipes save lives. Install a loud exhaust system to make sure car drivers and neighbours know you're coming.

ATGATT. All The Gear, All The Time. Riding gear is essential for safety, even on shorter trips.
